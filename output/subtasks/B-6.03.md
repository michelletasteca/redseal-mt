# B-6.03 | Stores products

**Major Work Activity:** B — Common Occupational Skills
**Task:** B-6 | Manages products

## Skills

### B-6.03.01P
**Performance Criteria:** ensure clean, organized and secure storage area

**Evidence of Attainment:** storage area is clean, organized and secure according to manufacturers’ specifications, jurisdictional regulations and company policies

### B-6.03.02P
**Performance Criteria:** select and use food storage containers

**Evidence of Attainment:** food storage containers are selected and used according to product and quantity to be stored

### B-6.03.03P
**Performance Criteria:** recognize and store hazardous products

**Evidence of Attainment:** hazardous products are recognized and stored according to manufacturers’ specifications, jurisdictional regulations and company policies

### B-6.03.04P
**Performance Criteria:** store products

**Evidence of Attainment:** products are stored in designated area according to jurisdictional regulations and company policies

### B-6.03.05P
**Performance Criteria:** date, label and rotate products

**Evidence of Attainment:** products are dated, labeled and rotated using FIFO method

### B-6.03.06P
**Performance Criteria:** unpack and break down received products

**Evidence of Attainment:** received products are unpacked and broken down in size as required and storage containers are used according to jurisdictional regulations

## Range of Variables

- storage areas include: refrigerators, freezers, dry storage, chemical storage
- jurisdictional regulations include: WHMIS, HACCP, OH&S, public health regulations
- food storage containers include: food-grade plastic, stainless steel, non-reactive, vacuum-packed, eco- friendly
- products include: dairy, produce, meats, poultry, seafood, dry goods, sundries

## Knowledge

### B-6.03.01L
**Learning Outcomes:** demonstrate knowledge of storing products

**Learning Objectives:**
- identify types of storage areas and describe their applications
- explain potential for cross-contamination between raw, cooked and ready-to-serve food
- explain methods of inventory control

### B-6.03.02L
**Learning Outcomes:** demonstrate knowledge of procedures used to store products

**Learning Objectives:**
- describe procedures used to label, date and rotate stock (FIFO)
- describe storage techniques for raw, cooked and ready-to-serve food products

### B-6.03.03L
**Learning Outcomes:** demonstrate knowledge of jurisdictional regulations pertaining to safe food storage practices

**Learning Objectives:**
- interpret jurisdictional regulations pertaining to safe food storage practices

## Range of Variables (Knowledge)

- products include: dairy, produce, meats, poultry, seafood, dry goods, sundries
- storage areas include: refrigerators, freezers, dry storage, chemical storage
- jurisdictional regulations include: WHMIS, HACCP, OH&S, public health regulations
