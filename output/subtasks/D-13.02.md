# D-13.02 | Processes stock and broth ingredients

**Major Work Activity:** D — Stocks, Broths and Soups
**Task:** D-13 | Prepares stocks and broths

## Skills

### D-13.02.01P
**Performance Criteria:** select and use tools and equipment

**Evidence of Attainment:** tools and equipment are selected and used according to recipe specifications

### D-13.02.02P
**Performance Criteria:** calculate quantity of ingredients

**Evidence of Attainment:** quantity of ingredients for yield are calculated according to recipe specifications

### D-13.02.03P
**Performance Criteria:** gather ingredients

**Evidence of Attainment:** individual ingredients are gathered and accessible in a sequential manner according to recipe specifications

### D-13.02.04P
**Performance Criteria:** measure ingredients

**Evidence of Attainment:** ingredients are measured according to recipe specifications

### D-13.02.05P
**Performance Criteria:** prepare ingredients

**Evidence of Attainment:** ingredients are prepared according to recipe specifications

## Range of Variables

- tools and equipment include: strainers, spigot pots, roasting pans, skimmers, stock pots, cutting boards, knives, scales, cheese cloth, butchers twine
- ingredients include: bones, vegetables, aromatics, water, proteins

## Knowledge

### D-13.02.01L
**Learning Outcomes:** demonstrate knowledge of stocks and broths and their applications

**Learning Objectives:**
- define terminology associated with stocks and broths
- identify types of stocks, and describe their characteristics, applications and procedures for use
- identify types of broths, and describe their characteristics, applications and procedures for use
- identify factors to consider when processing ingredients for stocks and broths
- identify ingredients and ratios used in stocks and broths
- identify dietary requirements specific to stocks and broths

### D-13.02.02L
**Learning Outcomes:** demonstrate knowledge of procedures used to process ingredients for stocks and broths

**Learning Objectives:**
- describe procedures used to process ingredients for stocks and broths

## Range of Variables (Knowledge)

- types of stocks include: white, brown, court-bouillon, fumet
- types of broths include: dashi, emulsif ied, clear, bone, miso
- factors include: freshness, cost, fat content, intended application, type of bone, cartilage content, managing trim, availability of ingredients
- ingredients include: bones, vegetables, aromatics, water, proteins
- dietary requirements include: cultural, allergies, ethical, religious, health-related, environmental, preferences
