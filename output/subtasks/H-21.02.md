# H-21.02 | Processes grains and pulses

**Major Work Activity:** H — Grains, Pulses, Seeds, Nuts and Alternative
**Task:** H-21 | Prepares grains and pulses

## Skills

### H-21.02.01P
**Performance Criteria:** inspect grains and pulses

**Evidence of Attainment:** grains and pulses are inspected to ensure they are free of contaminants

### H-21.02.02P
**Performance Criteria:** discard grains and pulses

**Evidence of Attainment:** grains and pulses that show appearance of chemical and biological contamination are discarded

### H-21.02.03P
**Performance Criteria:** wash grains and pulses

**Evidence of Attainment:** grains and pulses are thoroughly washed in cold water to remove extra starch and contaminants

### H-21.02.04P
**Performance Criteria:** strain grains and pulses

**Evidence of Attainment:** grains and pulses are strained to remove excess water

### H-21.02.05P
**Performance Criteria:** soak grains and pulses

**Evidence of Attainment:** grains and pulses are soaked to rehydrate and ensure even doneness according to type

## Range of Variables

- grains include: rice (short, long), wild rice, barley, bulgur, oats
- pulses include: lentils, beans, peas
- contaminants include: rocks, metal, twigs, leaves, insects

## Knowledge

### H-21.02.01L
**Learning Outcomes:** demonstrate knowledge of grains and pulses and their applications

**Learning Objectives:**
- define terminology associated with grains and pulses
- identify types of grains and pulses, and describe their characteristics and applications
- identify dietary requirements specific to grains and pulses
- identify soaking and washing requirements
- identify ratio of water to product for soaking

### H-21.02.02L
**Learning Outcomes:** demonstrate knowledge of procedures used to clean grains and pulses

**Learning Objectives:**
- describe procedures used to clean grains and pulses
- describe procedures used to wash grains and pulses
- describe procedures used, and length of time required, to soak grains and pulses

## Range of Variables (Knowledge)

- grains include: rice (short, long), wild rice, barley, bulgur, oats
- pulses include: lentils, beans, peas
- dietary requirements include: cultural, allergies, ethical, religious, health-related, environmental, preferences
