MAJOR WORK ACTIVITY H
Grains, Pulses, Seeds, Nuts and Alternative

TASK H-21 | Prepares grains and pulses
In order to produce a variety of quality grain and pulse dishes in both individual and larger quantities, it is important for cooks to be aware of preparation techniques, methods, and grain and pulse varieties and characteristics.

SUB-TASKS:

SUB-TASK H-21.01 | Selects grains and pulses

SKILLS:

- H-21.01.01P
  Performance Criteria: identify and choose types of grains and pulses
  Evidence of Attainment: types of grains and pulses are identified and chosen according to recipe specifications and desired results

- H-21.01.02P
  Performance Criteria: determine quantity of grains and pulses
  Evidence of Attainment: quantity of grains and pulses is determined according to required yield

- H-21.01.03P
  Performance Criteria: determine quality of grains and pulses
  Evidence of Attainment: quality of grains and pulses is determined by performing sensory assessment

RANGE OF VARIABLES:
- grains include: rice (short, long), wild rice, barley, bulgur, oats
- pulses include: lentils, beans, peas

KNOWLEDGE:

- H-21.01.01L
  Learning Outcomes: demonstrate knowledge of grains and pulses and their applications
  Learning Objectives:
  - define terminology associated with grains and pulses
  - identify types of grains and pulses, and describe their characteristics and applications
  - identify differences between processed and dried grains and pulses, and describe their characteristics and applications
  - identify dietary requirements specific to grains and pulses

- H-21.01.02L
  Learning Outcomes: demonstrate knowledge of procedures used to select grains and pulses
  Learning Objectives:
  - describe procedures used to select grains and pulses
  - describe methods to determine yields of grains and pulses
  - describe procedures used to handle grains and pulses

RANGE OF VARIABLES:
- grains include: rice (short, long), wild rice, barley, bulgur, oats
- pulses include: lentils, beans, peas
- dietary requirements include: cultural, allergies, ethical, religious, health-related, environmental, preferences

SUB-TASK H-21.02 | Processes grains and pulses

SKILLS:

- H-21.02.01P
  Performance Criteria: inspect grains and pulses
  Evidence of Attainment: grains and pulses are inspected to ensure they are free of contaminants

- H-21.02.02P
  Performance Criteria: discard grains and pulses
  Evidence of Attainment: grains and pulses that show appearance of chemical and biological contamination are discarded

- H-21.02.03P
  Performance Criteria: wash grains and pulses
  Evidence of Attainment: grains and pulses are thoroughly washed in cold water to remove extra starch and contaminants

- H-21.02.04P
  Performance Criteria: strain grains and pulses
  Evidence of Attainment: grains and pulses are strained to remove excess water

- H-21.02.05P
  Performance Criteria: soak grains and pulses
  Evidence of Attainment: grains and pulses are soaked to rehydrate and ensure even doneness according to type

RANGE OF VARIABLES:
- grains include: rice (short, long), wild rice, barley, bulgur, oats
- pulses include: lentils, beans, peas
- contaminants include: rocks, metal, twigs, leaves, insects

KNOWLEDGE:

- H-21.02.01L
  Learning Outcomes: demonstrate knowledge of grains and pulses and their applications
  Learning Objectives:
  - define terminology associated with grains and pulses
  - identify types of grains and pulses, and describe their characteristics and applications
  - identify dietary requirements specific to grains and pulses
  - identify soaking and washing requirements
  - identify ratio of water to product for soaking

- H-21.02.02L
  Learning Outcomes: demonstrate knowledge of procedures used to clean grains and pulses
  Learning Objectives:
  - describe procedures used to clean grains and pulses
  - describe procedures used to wash grains and pulses
  - describe procedures used, and length of time required, to soak grains and pulses

RANGE OF VARIABLES:
- grains include: rice (short, long), wild rice, barley, bulgur, oats
- pulses include: lentils, beans, peas
- dietary requirements include: cultural, allergies, ethical, religious, health-related, environmental, preferences

TASK H-23 | Prepares alternative proteins
Alternative proteins can be served on their own, as main dishes, or as a source of protein in many recipes. They can also be incorporated as key ingredients in many dishes such as soups, pastas, salads, and hot and cold appetizers. Alternative proteins may include insects and plant-based protein sources.

SUB-TASKS:

SUB-TASK H-23.01 | Selects alternative proteins

SKILLS:

- H-23.01.01P
  Performance Criteria: choose types of alternative proteins
  Evidence of Attainment: types of alternative proteins are chosen according to recipe specifications, desired results and dietary requirements

- H-23.01.02P
  Performance Criteria: choose amount of alternative proteins
  Evidence of Attainment: amount of alternative proteins are chosen according to recipe specifications

RANGE OF VARIABLES:
- alternative proteins include: tofu (firm, soft), tempeh, bean curd, seitan (gluten), market forms (prepared and packaged), insects
- dietary requirements include: cultural, allergies, ethical, religious, health-related, environmental, preferences

RANGE OF VARIABLES:
