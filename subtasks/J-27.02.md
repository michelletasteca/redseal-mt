# J-27.02 | Processes fin fish

**Major Work Activity:** J — Fish and Seafood
**Task:** J-27 | Prepares fin fish

## Skills

### J-27.02.01P
**Performance Criteria:** select and use processing tools and equipment

**Evidence of Attainment:** processing tools and equipment are selected and used according to recipe specifications and desired results

### J-27.02.02P
**Performance Criteria:** defrost fin fish

**Evidence of Attainment:** fin fish is defrosted maintaining quality and cell structure

### J-27.02.03P
**Performance Criteria:** prepare fin fish

**Evidence of Attainment:** fin fish is prepared using methods according to desired results and recipe specifications

### J-27.02.04P
**Performance Criteria:** minimize waste and utilize trim for other uses

**Evidence of Attainment:** waste is minimized and trim utilized for other uses

### J-27.02.05P
**Performance Criteria:** select seasonings

**Evidence of Attainment:** seasonings are selected according to recipe specifications

## Range of Variables

- tools and equipment include: scalers, knives, pliers, tweezers, cutting boards, scale
- fin fish includes: perch, pickerel, salmonids, cod, mahi mahi, halibut, snapper, sardines, tuna
- methods include: cleaning, cutting, slicing, scaling, trimming, de-boning, portioning, filleting, skinning, eviscerating, dressing, brining, marinating, curing, smoking
- other uses include: mousses, quenelles, patties, stock

## Knowledge

### J-27.02.01L
**Learning Outcomes:** demonstrate knowledge of fin fish and their applications

**Learning Objectives:**
- define terminology associated with processing fin fish
- identify hazards, and describe safe work practices and precautions pertaining to handling fin fish
- identify origin of fin fish, and describe their characteristics and applications
- identify factors to consider when processing fin fish
- identify dietary requirements specific to fin fish

### J-27.02.02L
**Learning Outcomes:** demonstrate knowledge of procedures used to process fin fish

**Learning Objectives:**
- describe procedures used to verify quality and cuts of fin fish using sensory assessment
- describe methods used to process fin fish

### J-27.02.03L
**Learning Outcomes:** demonstrate knowledge of procedures used to handle and store live and raw fin fish

**Learning Objectives:**
- describe procedures used to handle and store live and raw fin fish

## Range of Variables (Knowledge)

- fin fish includes: perch, pickerel, salmonids, cod, mahi mahi, halibut, snapper, sardines, tuna
- origin includes: wild, farmed, freshwater, saltwater
- dietary requirements include: cultural, allergies, ethical, religious, health-related, environmental, preferences
- methods include: cleaning, cutting, slicing, scaling, trimming, de-boning, portioning, filleting, skinning, eviscerating, dressing, brining, marinating, curing, s moking
